Who's in your story — and who isn't?

Look at who showed up. Is your family there? Friends? Coworkers? Are you completely alone? The people missing from your future are as telling as the ones you included.

What assumptions did you make about the world around you?

Did you assume things still work the same way they do now? What stayed the same that you didn't question? What changed that surprised you as you were writing?

What did you NOT write about — and what does that mean?

Every story has gaps. What topic, relationship, or problem was completely absent? The thing you didn't write is often the thing you're most confidently wrong about.
